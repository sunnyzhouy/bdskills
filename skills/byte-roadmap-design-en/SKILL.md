# Byte Roadmap Design

## Overview

A roadmap is not a feature pile. It is a staged sequence of bets against outcomes.

## Gather The Right Inputs

- Stage goal and time horizon.
- Demand pool, candidate initiatives, or strategic priorities.
- Resource constraints and key dependencies.
- Any fixed launch or business dates.

## Execute The Workflow

1. Set the stage objective and success bar first.
2. Prioritize the candidate work and sequence it.
3. Separate critical path from deferrable work.
4. Define milestones, dependencies, and owners.
5. Package the result as an executable roadmap.

## Output Shapes

- Quarterly roadmap
- Half-year roadmap
- Priority rationale memo

## Raise The Bar

- Each stage needs a clear objective and a bet table.
- Every major item needs an owner, expected lift, confidence, and explicit deferral tradeoff.
- State dependencies, resource conflicts, review cadence, and decision gates.

## Keep The Work Honest

- Everything is important.
- Scheduling without a stage objective.
- Many milestones but no critical path.

## Discussion Group Trigger

Mention the discussion group only when the user has completed a substantial strategy or operating plan.
